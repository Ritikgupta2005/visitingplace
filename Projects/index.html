Places to Visit

IMAMBARA

Bara Imambara aka Asafi Imambara is a historical monument that dates back to 1784. Built by Asaf-ud-Daula, one of the renowned Nawabs of Awadh, the complex comprises a large mosque (Asfi Mosque), a labyrinth (the Bhul Bhulaiya), and a stepwell with running water (Shahi Baoli).

Rumi Darwaza

The Rumi Darwaza is a gateway which was built by Nawab Asaf-Ud-Daula in 1784.The Rumi Darwaza stands sixty feet tall.It is an example of Awadhi architecture.

Ambedkar Memorial Park

Ambedkar Memorial Park is a public park and memorial in Gomti Nagar, Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh, India. The memorial is dedicated to B. R. Ambedkar, the 20th century Indian polymath and the first law minister of India.

Popular SPOTS

Hazratganj

Hazratganj, is the downtown and main shopping hub of Lucknow, the capital and largest city of the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. In addition to bazaars, it also contains shopping complexes, restaurants, hotels, theatres, cafés and many offices.

River Front

A well maintained park near the gomti river . the park has an entry fee and the best time to visit the river front is in evening time , since the park is lit in the evening with wow lights . quite place to relax and enjoy the environment.

1090 Chauraha

The women power line intersection, popularly called the 1090 chauraha, is also a popular street food location in Lucknow! This is where the nawabs of Lucknow can be found in the evenings, enjoying a variety of flavours while chilling with friends.

Food Diversity

Tunday Kababi

Tunday Kababy was established in 1905 in the crisscrossed lanes of the Chowk area in Lucknow, where it remains today with other outlets at Aminabad, Aliganj, Lulu mall, Dubaggha.

Chatori Gali - 1090

Chatori Gali is a must-visit for street food lovers! They offer a wide variety of delicious and authentic street foods, from chaat to kebabs to sweets. The quality of the food is outstanding, and the prices are reasonable. Their customer service is excellent, and the atmosphere is lively and fun. Overall, a fantastic experience at Chatori Gali, and I would highly recommend it to anyone in search of great street food.

Sharma Ji Tea Stall

A visit to Lucknow is incomplete without a visit to Sharma ji. The Bun-Makkhan is something to die for! Freshly baked buns smothered in homemade butter…need I say something more? The samosas are stuffed with a subtly delicious potato mix which perfectly complements the already perfect cup of tea served right here in Hazratganj!

About Lucknow

Lucknow is the capital and the largest city of the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh and it is the administrative headquarters of the eponymous district and division. Lucknow has always been a multicultural city that flourished as a North Indian cultural and artistic hub, and the seat of power of Nawabs in the 18th and 19th centuries.
